# Problem 2 – Energy of a harmonic oscillator

Given:

- \(m=1\ \text{kg}\)
- \(k=100\ \text{N/m}\)
- \(x(0)=2\ \text{m}\)
- \(v(0)=1\ \text{m/s}\)

---

## 1. Natural frequency

For a spring-mass oscillator:

$$
\omega=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

Substitute the data:

$$
\omega=\sqrt{\frac{100}{1}}=10\ \text{rad/s}
$$

The ordinary frequency is:

$$
f=\frac{\omega}{2\pi}=\frac{10}{2\pi}=\frac{5}{\pi}\ \text{Hz}
$$

Approximate value:

$$
f\approx 1.59\ \text{Hz}
$$

---

## 2. Total energy

The total energy is

$$
E=T+U
$$

where

$$
T=\frac12 mv^2,\qquad U=\frac12 kx^2
$$

Using the initial values:

$$
E=\frac12\cdot 1\cdot 1^2+\frac12\cdot 100\cdot 2^2
$$

$$
E=0.5+50\cdot 4
$$

$$
E=0.5+200=200.5\ \text{J}
$$

---

## 3. For what displacement does kinetic energy equal 50% of total energy?

We want:

$$
T=\frac12 E
$$

Therefore:

$$
U=E-T=\frac12 E
$$

So potential energy must also be half of total energy:

$$
\frac12 kx^2=\frac12 E
$$

Multiply both sides by 2:

$$
kx^2=E
$$

Thus:

$$
x^2=\frac{E}{k}
$$

$$
x=\pm\sqrt{\frac{E}{k}}
$$

Substitute:

$$
x=\pm\sqrt{\frac{200.5}{100}}
$$

$$
x=\pm\sqrt{2.005}
$$

Approximate value:

$$
x\approx \pm 1.42\ \text{m}
$$

---

## Final results

Natural angular frequency:

$$
\omega=10\ \text{rad/s}
$$

Natural frequency:

$$
f=\frac{5}{\pi}\approx 1.59\ \text{Hz}
$$

Total energy:

$$
E=200.5\ \text{J}
$$

Displacement where kinetic energy is 50% of total energy:

$$
x=\pm\sqrt{\frac{200.5}{100}}\approx \pm 1.42\ \text{m}
$$
