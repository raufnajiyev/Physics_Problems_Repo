# Problem 8 – Relative motion

Given:

$$
\vec v_A=(3,1),\qquad \vec v_B=(1,-2)
$$

---

## 1. Relative velocity \( \vec v_{A/B} \)

The velocity of A relative to B is:

$$
\vec v_{A/B}=\vec v_A-\vec v_B
$$

Substitute:

$$
\vec v_{A/B}=(3,1)-(1,-2)
$$

$$
\vec v_{A/B}=(2,3)
$$

---

## 2. Direction of relative motion

The direction is given by the vector

$$
\vec v_{A/B}=(2,3)
$$

Its slope is:

$$
\frac{3}{2}
$$

The angle with respect to the \(x\)-axis is:

$$
\theta=\arctan\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)
$$

So body A moves relative to body B in the direction of the vector \((2,3)\).

---

## 3. Interpretation in different reference frames

### a) Reference frame attached to the origin

- Body A moves with velocity \((3,1)\)
- Body B moves with velocity \((1,-2)\)

### b) Reference frame attached to body A

In this frame, body A is at rest.

The velocity of B relative to A is:

$$
\vec v_{B/A}=\vec v_B-\vec v_A
$$

$$
\vec v_{B/A}=(-2,-3)
$$

### c) Reference frame attached to body B

In this frame, body B is at rest.

The velocity of A relative to B is:

$$
\vec v_{A/B}=(2,3)
$$

---

## Final results

Relative velocity of A with respect to B:

$$
\vec v_{A/B}=(2,3)
$$

Relative velocity of B with respect to A:

$$
\vec v_{B/A}=(-2,-3)
$$

Direction angle:

$$
\theta=\arctan\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)
$$
